¬∂Contact lost with India's Vikram lander just 2.1 km above the lunar surface. The Vikram lander, with its onboard rover, was designed to function for 14 Earth days (1 lunar daytime). Its status is still being studied by India‚Äôs ISRO. Multiple reports were published on Monday that unreleased imaging shows the lander intact, but lying on its side. The Chandrayaan-2 orbiter is functioning nominally and will continue its planned 7-year scientific mission, including imaging with its 0.3 m/pixel resolution camera, the highest in lunar orbit.
