¬∂KPLO! South Korea‚Äôs first interplanetary mission, and the beginning of the country‚Äôs serious space ambitions, KPLO launches for the Moon in August 2022. The mission carries five instruments: a high-resolution imager, a wide-angle polarimetric camera which can determine surface materials based on the way light reflects and scatters, a gamma-ray spectrometer for determining elemental composition, a magnetometer, and ShadowCam, provided by NASA, which will peer inside permanently shadowed craters via scattered light using a sensor 200 times more sensitive than the camera on Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter. Also on board is a joint experiment with NASA to test resilient communication.
