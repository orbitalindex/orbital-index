  • What we thought we knew about type Ia supernovae may be wrong. White dwarfs accreting mass from a companion until crossing the Chandrasekhar limit is out; surface novas resulting in compression waves that trigger core detonation are in (paper). This could explain why type Ia supernovae have varying brightness, their high observed occurrence even with few white dwarfs thought to be close to the Chandrasekhar mass limit, and even explain an origin for some hypervelocity stars.
