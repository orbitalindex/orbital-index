Carrying an impressive deployable 12-meter-wide antenna on a 9-meter boom, NISAR will map the planet‚Äôs surface twice every 12 days with two different SAR bands: L-Band with an instrument from JPL, good at moisture, biomass, and land motion detection, and S-Band from ISRO, good at sensing changes to agriculture, grassland ecosystems, and infrastructure. Combined, these measurements will provide change detection at centimeter resolution and be invaluable for monitoring land motion (earthquakes, landslides, volcanic activity), ice shelves, erosion, as well as forest and wetland ecosystems. NISAR orbits in a 747 km SSO and is expected to generate 80 terabytes of data products per day, including polar regions in the Southern Hemisphere not covered by other SAR satellites today.
