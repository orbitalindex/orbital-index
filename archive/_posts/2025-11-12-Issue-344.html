¬∂Shenzhou takes a hit. Just as the new three-person Shenzhou-21 crew arrived at Tiangong station for their six-month stay, the return of the Shenzhou-20 crew, which they were meant to relieve, was delayed to assess the safety of their return craft after a suspected space debris impact. The Shenzhou-20 crew has already spent six months on the station, which is only designed to support more than three taikonauts for short periods. China does have the Shenzhou-22 capsule on standby and could launch it in short order (possible as soon as eight days), allowing the S-20 crew to return on S-21‚Äôs craft and a replacement to arrive shortly after for S-21‚Äôs eventual return.
