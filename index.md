After <a href="https://spaceflightnow.com/2024/02/05/live-coverage-spacex-to-launch-nasas-pace-mission-on-falcon-9-rocket-from-cape-canaveral/" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">weather delayed launch for two days</a>, a <a href="https://apnews.com/article/climate-satellite-launch-nasa-b9f5e8874e56f6166aa88ddc5689ff47" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">Falcon 9 successfully carried</a> NASA‚Äôs <a href="https://pace.gsfc.nasa.gov/" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">PACE (Plankton, Aerosol, Cloud, ocean Ecosystem)</a> satellite into orbit. PACE‚Äôs <a href="https://pace.oceansciences.org/oci.htm" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">primary instrument</a> will measure the color of the Earth‚Äôs oceans from ultraviolet to shortwave infrared, delivering ‚Äúunprecedented coverage‚Äù from 350 nm to 2,260 nm. PACE also carries <a href="https://pace.oceansciences.org/spexone.htm" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">two</a> <a href="https://pace.oceansciences.org/harp2.htm" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">polarimeters</a> to measure how clouds, aerosols, and the ocean affect light. Data from these instruments combined will provide information about ocean and atmosphere carbon dioxide exchange, algal blooms, the effects of aerosols on phytoplankton growth, and the evolution of our oceans‚Äô biogeochemical systems. The 1,700 kg spacecraft has onboard storage for 1.7 terabytes of data (which is only 7 orbits worth) and can support downlink at up to 600 Mbps. While it‚Äôs designed for a three-year primary mission, NASA expects it to last much longer, with at least a decade of propellant onboard.</p>

Each satellite carries a thermal infrared spectrometer, originally developed for MRO‚Äôs <a href="https://mars.nasa.gov/mro/mission/instruments/mcs/" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">Mars Climate Sounder</a> instrument, and since miniaturized and updated for the warmer atmosphere and tinier payload requirements of PREFIRE. Polar regions help regulate the planet‚Äôs warm topics where most solar radiation is absorbed; circular prevailing weather patterns carry tropical heat to the poles where it is radiated as infrared energy back into space. This polar process hasn‚Äôt been directly measured at wavelengths over 15 Œºm which account for nearly 60% of emitted radiation. Data from PREFIRE about emissions from different polar surface types and conditions will help fill this gap for global climate models‚Äîparticularly important as polar regions change (<a href="https://journals.ametsoc.org/view/journals/bams/102/7/BAMS-D-20-0155.1.xml" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">paper</a>).</p>
